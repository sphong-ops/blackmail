Let O = (0, 0), A = (1/2, 0), and B = (0, sqrt(3)/2) be points in the coordinate plane. Let F be the family of segments PQ of unit length lying in the first quadrant with P on the x-axis and Q on the y-axis. There is a unique point C on segment AB, distinct from A and B, that does not belong to any segment from F other than segment AB. Then OC^2 = p/q where p and q are relatively prime positive integers. Find p + q.
